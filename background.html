Timeline

From Encyclopedia Britannica.

1953

Cambodia becomes independent from French rule.

1970

Prince Sihanouk removed from power in favor of Lom Nol, prompting a civil war.

1970

Cambodia invaded by United States.

1975

Lom Nol's government collapses. The Khmer Rouge takes over, and the Communist Party of Kampuchea abolishes money, closes schools and prohibits publication of documents.

1976

Sihanouk resigns, Pol Pot takes office, Cambodia becomes Democratic Kampuchea.

1975-1979

At least 1.5 million Cambodians die from overwork, starvation, disease, or execution.

1978

Vietnam invades, installs the People's Republic of Kampuchea.

1978-1988

At least 500,000 Cambodians flee to Thailand or other countries.

1982

Sihanouk establishes government-in-exile.

1992

Private ownership is allowed.

1993

Elections held under auspices of United Nations, royalists win with 90 percent voter turnout.

Mid-1990's

Khmer Rouge collapses.

1997

Hun Sen stages a successful coup.

1998

Pol Pot dies.

1999

Cambodia admitted into ASEAN.

2004

Cambodia joins the World Trade Organization. Sihanouk resigns, his son, Norodom Sihamoni, succeeds him.

2010

First Khmer Rouge member found guilty of crimes against humanity.

2013

Mass protests occur due to political deadlock.

2017

Government dissolves CNRP party

Government

Local Name Preahreacheanachakr Kampuchea (ព្រះរាជាណាចក្រកម្ពុជា), literally "The Kingdom of Cambodia"

Goverment type Parliamentary constituational monarchy

Capital Phnom Penh

Suffrage 18 years, universal

Citizenship By descendance only

Chief of State King Norodom Sihamoni since October 2004

Prime Minister Hun Sen since January 1985

Legislative branch Bicameral. Senate has 61 seats with six year terms and representatives are chosen by parliamentarians, and the National Assembly has 123 seats with five year terms and members directly voted on through proportional representation vote.

Political Parties Cambodian People's Party. Their chief opponent, Cambodia National Rescue Party, was dissolved in 2017 by the government after accusations of plotting to overthrow Prime Minister Hun Sen.

Judicial Branch Supreme council, appellate court, provincial and municipal courts.

Conflicts and Disputes

Dissolution of CNRP (BBC) In 2017, Prime Minister Hun Sen dissolved the main opposition party under claims that it was part of an international plot to overthrow him. Hun Sen has been prime minister for 32 years, and served under Pol Pot. The members of the Cambodian National Rescue Party cannot serve for five years, and were making progress in the polls before they were banned. Elections are occuring in July.

Laos Cambodia is concerned about Laos' construction of upstream dams.

Vietnam Cambodia accuses Vietnam of illicit border crossings, and there are fights over offshore island ownership.

Thailand Continuous fight over ownership of Preah Vihear Temple ruins. They were awarded to Cambodia by the International Court of Justice in 1962, but both countries had a military standoff over the area in 2011.

Human Trafficking 256,800 estimated to be living in slavery. Ranked 3 out of 267 countries for modern slavery.
